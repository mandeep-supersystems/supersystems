Why steps repeat: QMS/Warehouse appears three times (incoming check, material issue, and final/outgoing check) because quality and stock control are needed at every material hand-off, not just once. Sales opens the loop (order booking) and closes it (invoice). Process Control and Production/Testing are separate because "setting up correctly" and "actually making it" are two different jobs, even though they happen back-to-back.
These four checkpoints are the only places in the whole flow where a formal Yes / No decision is made before work is allowed to continue.
Gate 1 of 4

IQC — Incoming Quality Control

Like checking grocery items at the door before putting them in your kitchen.

When material arrives from a vendor, it's checked against the drawing/spec before it's allowed into the stores. Nothing unchecked gets used in production.

YES → goes into storesNO → sent back to vendor
Gate 2 of 4

IPQC — In-Process Quality Control

Like tasting the food while cooking, not just when it's served.

While the production line is running, samples are checked regularly. This catches a problem after a few pieces, instead of after the whole batch is done.

YES → line keeps runningNO → line stops & is corrected
Gate 3 of 4

FQC — Final Quality Control

Like a final look in the mirror before leaving the house.

Every finished unit is checked one last time against the complete list of requirements before it's treated as a "good", sellable product.

YES → goes to finished-goods storeNO → sent back for rework/repair
Gate 4 of 4

OQC — Outgoing Quality Control

Like double-checking your bag has your ticket and passport before you leave for the airport.

Right before dispatch, someone confirms the correct items, correct quantity, correct packing, and correct paperwork are all in place.

YES → released for dispatchNO → shipment held back
Short, plain-English meanings for the short forms used in the diagram — no technical background needed.
BOM
Bill of Material — the full list of parts/ingredients needed to make one unit. Think of it like a recipe.
ECN
Engineering Change Notice — an official note saying "the design has changed, use this version instead of the old one."
MRP
Material Requirement Planning — a calculation that works out what material is needed, how much, and by when.
Work Order
An internal instruction telling the shop floor exactly what to make, how many, and by when.
QMS
Quality Management System — the overall team/system responsible for making sure every quality check actually happens properly.
IQC
Incoming Quality Control — checking material as it arrives from a vendor, before accepting it into stores.
IPQC
In-Process Quality Control — checking quality while the product is still being made, not just at the end.
FQC
Final Quality Control — the last check on a finished unit before it's treated as good and ready for stock.
OQC
Outgoing Quality Control — the very last check, right before goods leave the building for the customer.
Rework
Fixing a faulty unit so it becomes acceptable, instead of scrapping it entirely.
